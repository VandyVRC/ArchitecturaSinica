心間 xīnjiān

https://architecturasinica.org/keyword/k000142
Print   TEI/XML  

Terms

  • xinjian (Pinyin without tones)
  • 心間 (Traditional Chinese)
  • 心间 (Simplified Chinese)
  • xīnjiān (Pinyin)1
  • hsin-chien (Wade-Giles)
  • central bay (ATTCAT preferred translation) (English)2

Note (full)

    Literally “heart” or “central” “bay” (Kroll 2015, 507; 196; Steinhardt 1991, 27-50). According to the Yingzao fashi, xinjian (also called dangxinjian 當心間) refers to the central bay of a building, divided longitudinally by the two central front pillars (pingzhu 平柱) and covering the entire depth (YZFS 5.7a). Occasionally, it may also refer to the unit of space between these two pillars (YZFS 4.9a-b;5.4b). According to the Yingzao fashi, there should only be one xinjian in any given building. 3

    As an architectural term, xinjian was adopted only in official texts of the Song dynasty and was interchangeable with zhongjian 中間 (literally center, or central, bay). In the Republican period, alongside the study of the Yingzao fashi, scholars of the Yingzao xueshe adopted xinjian as a basic architectural term equated with mingjian 明間 (Liang 1932, 29).5

    心間 (亦作當心間)，在《營造法式》中指位于建築中央，以前檐中心二柱（平柱）為界，包含整個進深的基本單元 (李 2009，5.7a)。也存在僅指前檐二柱之間的情況 (李 2009, 4.9a-b;5.4b)。從 《營造法式》的語境看，一座建築物的“心間（當心間）”有且僅有一個。4

    “心間”在历史文獻中僅見於宋代帶有官方性質的文献，并存在與“中間”並用的情況。在民国时期，隨著營造學社對《營造法式》的研究，“心間”被重新引入到現代建築史研究中，成為“明間” 的同義词 (梁 1932，29)。5
