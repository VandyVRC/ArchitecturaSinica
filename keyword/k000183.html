草架 cǎojià

https://architecturasinica.org/keyword/k000183
Print   TEI/XML  

Terms

  • caojia (Pinyin without tones)
  • 草架 (Traditional Chinese)
  • 草架 (Simplified Chinese)
  • cǎojià (Pinyin)1
  • ts'ao-chia (Wade-Giles)
  • rough structural frame (English)2
  • draft frame (lit.) (English)3

Note

    As an architectural term, caojia (literally "draft frame," Kroll 2015, 34, 194), primarily appears in technical manuals such as Yingzao fashi (1103), Yuanye 園冶 (1631), and Yingzao fayuan 營造法原 (1959). It refers to the roof frame between the interior ceiling (pingqi 平棊 or caojing 藻井 in Yingzao Fashi and xuan 軒 or juan 卷 in mansion-hall (tingtang 廳堂) style architecture of the Jiangnan area during the Ming and Qing dynasties) and the roof, which is left rough (hence the term "draft") because it is not visible to the viewer (Chen 2010, 259; Yao 1986, 116).4

    In addition, the term caojia zhuzi 草架柱子 in Qing dynasty documents has a similar meaning of a "rough pillar" because, like a draft, it was not meant to exposed (Gongbu 1734, 2.21a; Guan 1644-1911, 29; Li 1795, 17.6a).4

    草架作為建築術語，主要出現在《營造法式》（1103）、《園冶》（1631）及《營造法原》（1959）中。指室內天花（即《營造法式》中的平棊或藻井； 明代江南廳堂中的軒（卷））與屋面之間的屋架（陳 2010，259；姚 1986，116），因不露明而加工簡略。3

    此外，清代文獻中也有類似用法，以“草架柱子”表明其為不露明構件而做工簡略（工部 1734, 2.21a； 官 1644-1911, 29； 李 1795, 17.6a)。3
